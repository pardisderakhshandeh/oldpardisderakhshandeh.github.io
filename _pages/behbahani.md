## About Behbahani
Behbahani is an endangered Southwestern Iranian language spoken primarily in the city of Behbahan, Iran. As a primarily oral language without a standardized writing system, linguistic documentation and formal analysis are crucial for its preservation and study.

Typologically, Behbahani presents unique puzzles for theoretical syntax and morphology. It exhibits split alignment and complex verbal agreement systems, notably completely lacking morphological case. 

## Research & Documentation
My work focuses on formally capturing the syntactic structure of Behbahani, particularly asking how much of what appears as surface morphology is driven by deeper syntactic operations like argument licensing and Voice projections.
