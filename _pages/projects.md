## Computational Modeling of Behbahani Agreement

To complement my theoretical syntactic analysis, I am developing computational models to formally capture Behbahani agreement and alignment patterns. 

Using Haskell, I am building finite-state automata (FSA) designed to parse and generate the complex interactions between tense, transitivity, and stem choice in a system lacking morphological case. This project bridges formal syntactic theory with functional programming to test the architectural limits of split alignment systems. 

## Research Mentorship: "Linguists, Meet Behbahani"

Beyond formal analysis, I am invested in the documentation and visibility of Behbahani. 

I recently organized and directed a collaborative research initiative culminating in a dedicated session at the Annual Meeting of the Linguistic Society of America (LSA). For this project, I led and trained a team of ten undergraduate researchers, guiding them through the process of processing and preparing primary linguistic data to introduce the Behbahani variety to the broader linguistic community.
